    The two showcase channels here move fast. Good projects sink below the scroll line in days, and tracking
     down that one skill or plugin you half-remember means paging through hundreds of forum threads.
    
     So I built a registry: a static site that auto-syncs from the Community Projects Showcase and
     Plugins/Skills/Skins channels every Monday, with client-side search and filters over the whole catalog.
    Built with Hermes Agent, of course.
    
     🔗  https://babylondreams.de/hermes-registry
    
     **What's in it right now**
     • **496** entries, pulled from 600+ Discord forum threads
     • Types: `skill` · `plugin` · `tool` · `agent` · `skin` · `integration`
     • Categories: memory, productivity, ui, devops, observability, security, voice, creative, and more
    
     **How to use it**
     • Search across name, description, and author as you type
     • Type filter chips + a category dropdown narrow it further
     • `/` focuses search, `Esc` clears it — built keyboard-first
    
     **How it stays fresh**
     Three jobs run every Monday morning: scrape the showcases, classify and categorize the new items
     (deterministic heuristics first, an LLM pass for the ambiguous ones), then rebuild and deploy. The Discord
     is the source of truth; there's no manual entry list to maintain.
    
     **Honest notes**
     • Community-maintained — not affiliated with Nous Research.
     • Descriptions come from each repo, so some are sparse. That's the community's data, shown as-is rather
     than dressed up.
     • If your project is missing, mistyped, or in the wrong category, reply here or open an issue and it'll get
     folded in on the next sync.
    
     If you build on Hermes, this is meant to save you the scroll.
