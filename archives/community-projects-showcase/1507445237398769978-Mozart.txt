    Hey everyone,
    
    I’m building an open-source project called Mozart.
    
    Mozart is not another LLM gateway.
    
    The idea is simple: most AI builders already use tools like OpenCode, OpenClaw, Hermes, Cursor, LiteLLM, OpenRouter, Ollama, local models, cloud providers, etc.
    
    But once you use multiple models/providers, the real pain becomes:
    
    - choosing the right model for each task,
    - avoiding unnecessary premium calls,
    - dealing with quotas and rate limits,
    - keeping sensitive context local,
    - switching providers without breaking workflows,
    - understanding why a model was selected.
    
    Mozart is designed as a local conductor for AI agents.
    
    It integrates as a skill, tool, adapter or lightweight middleware into existing gateways/agents.
    It does not try to replace gateways or duplicate what they already do.
    
    The core principle is:
    
    Gateways execute. Mozart decides.
    
    Mozart aims to:
    
    - detect your existing AI stack,
    - read available providers/models from your current setup,
    - route tasks based on cost, context, latency, privacy and quotas,
    - protect sensitive context,
    - explain every routing decision,
    - work locally and transparently.
    
    The project is open-source and available here:
    
    https://github.com/ucav/mozart-router
    
    It’s still early, but the goal is to build something genuinely useful for agent builders.
    
    I’d love feedback from people working with agents, local/cloud models, gateways, routing, OpenCode/OpenClaw/Hermes-style workflows, or multi-provider setups.
    
    What would you expect from a tool like this?
