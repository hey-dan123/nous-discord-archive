    Teams constantly update and share skills. Then we empower agents to spend on them — pay vendors, buy compute, settle invoices. Two problems nobody's solving:
    
    Use case 1 -> We all now share skills. One skill, many teams, different truths. The skill version one team is happy with, another team in the same org is not - and both can be right. Blindly shipping "the latest" to everyone is how the wrong version authorizes a payment. SkillOps certifies **per team**: the exact same bytes can be authorized for Procurement and refused for Finance, by policy, not by accident.
    
    Use case 2 -> Self-improving agents that spend. **Hermes auto-improves a skill that's wired to move money**. That improvement has to be evaluated before it's allowed to pay - money-spenders need that. SkillOps re-runs that team's eval and lets Stripe capture only if the exact version running right now is certified; otherwise it voids the charge and heals back to the last good version.
    
    The version it checks is a SHA measured from disk inside the decision, so the agent can't fake what it's running. And every decision is a hash-chained, tamper-evident audit row. Fail-closed by construction.
    
    Built on Nous Hermes × NVIDIA (Nemotron + NemoClaw) × Stripe. Hackathon submission. Now updated.
