    TL;DR
    Install: hermes plugins install DanielLi202/hermes-tag
    Repo: https://github.com/DanielLi202/hermes-tag
    
    Run a Hermes bot in a busy group and you hit one of two problems: it answers off your last message alone, or it dumps the whole history into the model. Hermes Tag is the in-between.
    
    You @ it, and it replies in the thread using that chat's bounded memory plus the evidence that actually matters: the images someone posted, the notes around them, the replies that relate. Not just your last line. Not the whole backlog. It stays silent until you mention it.
    
    It's a context-selection layer sitting over Hermes's Feishu platform; Slack runs through the same seam. Instead of full-history RAG it picks a scope per message (focused_reply, deictic_recent, plain), keeps memory per-chat so nothing leaks between groups, and gives admins an audit trail — /tag admin audit is redacted, and the allowlist is the only thing it stores from.
    
    Real recording : three charts get posted, some notes and chatter, then a late @, and it answers from exactly those.
    
    It's early (v0.3.x). On Feishu you'll need an org admin to grant one scope (im:message.group_msg) for full background context — without it, @-replies still work but it can't see unmentioned messages. Glad to help if anyone wants it running in their group.

    v0.4.0 — the "why is the bot ignoring my group" release
    
    The most common failure report I got wasn't a crash. It was silence: you add the bot to a group, the config says it can read group messages, and it just... doesn't. Almost every time, the Feishu app never actually got the im:message.group_msg grant — the config claimed it, the app didn't hold it, and nothing anywhere told you.
    
    So now the plugin checks. Once per process it verifies the config's claim against what the app actually holds (the probe itself needs no extra permission). On a mismatch it logs, writes an audit event, and DMs the admins once, with the fix spelled out. /tag status shows the verdict: capability_check=ok|mismatch|upgrade_available|unknown.
    
    Also in this release:
    
    sender identity is normalized to open_id at ingress, so admin checks and stored authors stay stable even when the employee-id scope is granted
    a known-limits page mapping "bot looks broken" symptoms to what's actually a Feishu-platform or hermes-core limit (connection, rate limits, media, account types): https://github.com/DanielLi202/hermes-tag/blob/main/docs/known-limits.md
