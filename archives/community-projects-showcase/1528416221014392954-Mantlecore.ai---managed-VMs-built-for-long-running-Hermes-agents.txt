    First of all, thank you to all the community members who helped us with the private beta testing! ❤️ 
    
    We've spent the last 5 months building cloud infrastructure specifically for open-source AI agents, for anyone who wants Hermes running 24/7 without owning the machine it lives on.
    
    Two barriers kept coming up in our conversations with agent users:
    - **Non-technical users** hit a wall before the agent gets its first task - servers, Docker, networking, SSL, updates.
    - **Technical users** discover deployment is the easy part. A long-running Hermes accumulates memory, skills, files, and OAuth integrations, and one bad update, or one of the agent's own self-modifications, can wipe out weeks of accumulated state.
    
    Mantlecore solves both:
    → Hermes runs in a dedicated VM with kernel-level isolation, not a shared container, no noisy neighbors
    → Persistent storage for memory, skills, and integrations
    → Snapshot before anything risky, one-click restore, daily off-site backups
    → Bring your own API keys or oAuth subscriptions, or use our managed LLM access
    
    A self-improving agent connected to 18+ platforms is exactly the kind of thing you don't want to lose to its own experiments. The idea: let the agent evolve freely, and if something goes sideways, roll back to a known-good state in minutes. Self-hosting control, minus the ops.
    
    We're in beta, and I'd genuinely value input from people who run Hermes seriously:
    -What would stop you from trusting a cloud platform with a self-modifying agent?
    -What failure mode worries you most with a long-running instance?
    
    There's a 7-day trial at https://mantlecore.ai - break things and tell me what broke. Follow the build at @mantlecoreai on X.
    
    *Mantlecore is independent and not affiliated with the Hermes project.*
