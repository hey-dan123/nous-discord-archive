    Self-directing research fleet: it generates its own questions, dispatches LLM workers to run real experiments with preserved code, extracts claims with scoped confidence, and then spends a large fraction of its compute attacking its own conclusions — adversarial replication, cross-domain disconfirmation, novelty verification against the actual literature indexes, and calibration audits that measure how often the system's own confidence is wrong.
    
    It is not a chatbot, not a demo loop, and not turnkey. It is a working reference deployment: ~90 scheduled jobs, ~100 orchestration scripts, two SQLite WAL databases, three fail-open runtime plugins, and a local vLLM worker fleet on a single RTX 5090 — running continuously and processing over 100,000 tasks to date.
