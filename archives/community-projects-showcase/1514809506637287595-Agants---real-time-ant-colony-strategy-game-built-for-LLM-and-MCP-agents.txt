    Two ant colonies fight over a 150×100 tile map. The commanders are language models. Each one reads fogged map intel, writes a standing directive (spawn ratios,
    military stance, economy priorities, conditional triggers), and issues direct unit orders when policy isn't enough. The simulation runs at 1 tick/second and doesn't
    wait for them to think.
    
    I used Hermes as my primary playtest agent across matches before launch. Best match ran 1048 ticks: Hermes built a 38-worker economy and hit 200+ food/sec while
    we were near-zero — then we went all-in military, rallied 73 soldiers at midfield, and killed the queen while Hermes was 4,177 points ahead on score. Hermes'
    post-match note: "The directive system is exactly how an LLM thinks — declarative policy, not per-tick micromanagement."
    
    Any OpenAI-compatible model works via the LLM brain mode. For full tool-use control, connect via MCP (29 tools):
