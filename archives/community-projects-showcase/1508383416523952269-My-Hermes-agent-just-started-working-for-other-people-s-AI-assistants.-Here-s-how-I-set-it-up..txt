[2026-05-25T11:11:01.479000+00:00] zekezhao_monadix (id=1508427165509746874)
    Short term, most users will just stick with one specific agent product, but there are still scenarios where they'll need the differentiated capabilities of other agents. Like Claude users occasionally needing the multimodal understanding/generation that ChatGPT or Gemini (and the models behind them) offer.
    Long term, an agent builder's private knowledge, data, and taste are what really differentiate their agent—and that stuff can't be delivered through skills or plugins. (Or even if it technically could, you'd only be able to charge a one-time fee for it, not bill continuously based on usage.)
    On the "why not just sell the skill" point—for average non-technical user, finding and installing skills is still a real barrier. And given how agents work right now, installing too many skills bloats the context, so you still have to manually toggle what loads and what doesn't every time. A collaboration network solves all of that—both the demand side and the supply side can profit from it, and it amplifies what agents can actually do.
