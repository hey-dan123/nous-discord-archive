    i'm working on Potaco, a CLI for image generation and editing across multiple AI providers (OpenAI, fal, Vercel AI Gateway, or any OpenAI-compatible endpoint)
    
    why use Potaco over the built-in tool?
    - **image editing** — inpainting with masks (white=edit, black=keep), rectangular/circular masks, and outpainting to extend a canvas. The built-in tool only does generation.
    - **per-request control** — `--size`, `--quality`, `--n`, `--seed`, `--guidance-scale`, `--negative-prompt` for fine-tuned results.
    - **multi-provider** — switch between OpenAI, fal, Vercel, or a custom OpenAI-compatible endpoint with `potaco use <provider>`. Pick whatever fits your budget or quality needs.
    - **model discovery** — `potaco models` lists and picks available models from the active provider so you're not locked to one.
    - **reproducibility** — `--seed` for consistent results and `--dry-run` to inspect the request payload without spending credits.
    - **agent-friendly** — `--json` output, `--stdout` for piping raw bytes, and `--non-interactive` for agents.
    - **encrypted credentials** stored locally — no plain-text API keys on disk.
    
    still in beta, so there are rough edges. feedback and contributions welcome!
    
    check it out: https://github.com/ncxton/potaco
