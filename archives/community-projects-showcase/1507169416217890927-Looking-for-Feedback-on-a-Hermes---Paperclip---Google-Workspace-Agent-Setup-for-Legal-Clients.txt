    Hi everyone, I’m Pablo.
    
    I’m working on an automation project for a law firm in Guadalajara. The client liked using Hermes as the foundation, so I open-sourced the template as **We Law OS**:
    
    https://github.com/cuentadeservicio377-cell/we-law-open-source
    
    The goal is to turn a law firm’s knowledge, workflows, intake, document production, and team structure into an agentic legal operations system a lawyer can actually use.
    
    The architecture is:
    
    - the lawyer remains the firm owner / managing partner;
    - Hermes acts as the managing partner reflection and orchestration layer;
    - Paperclip acts as structured legal staff;
    - Google Workspace is the familiar office surface;
    - the dashboard is the human trust layer.
    
    The part I’m struggling with is integration quality. Hermes alone can produce strong legal documentation, especially when combined with Kami-style editorial/PDF rendering. But when I combine Hermes + Paperclip + autonomous worker delegation, the system is harder to control. Workers sometimes skip context, produce shallow outputs, or mark work done before the legal package is truly ready.
    
    So my main question is:
    
    **Am I overcomplicating the architecture?**
    
    Should Hermes remain the main orchestrator? Should Paperclip own more of the workflow, or only act as the org chart / staff ledger? Should I focus more on Hermes profiles, a stronger Legal Brain, stricter command packets, or deterministic validators around evidence maps, cross-document review, and final document rendering?
    
    I’m attaching a longer technical explanation with the full architecture and the problems I’m trying to solve.
    
    I’d really appreciate feedback from anyone working with Hermes, Paperclip, multi-agent systems, legal tech, document automation, Google Workspace automation, or agentic workflows.
