    Why LLMs write in your native language but still sound "sterile":
    
    Most models default to planning their concepts in English within their hidden Chain-of-Thought (thinking tokens), only translating to your target language (like Vietnamese) at the very last step. This latent translation layout strips out natural rhythm and phrasing.
    
    I solved this on my Hermes Agent setup by building a skill that forces the thinking tokens to execute natively in the target language from character zero. If the model drafts its thoughts in the native language directly, the resulting output matches the natural flow of a native writer—no translation step, no rigid grammar patterns. 
    
    Tested this across 10 technical and narrative drafts, and the raw output required zero manual rewriting.
    
    The breakdown and skill config: https://thanhan.dev/en/cases/vietnamese-writer-agent
