    For anyone who works with character design — or just keeps getting generic image-gen results — I published a set of skills for locking characters down and iterating on them.
    
        Whether you’re newer to this or already deep in it, and whether you give the agent image tools or not, there should be something useful here. 
    
        Three independent skills — use whichever matches where you are:
        1. Seed → bible — turn a rough idea or pile of notes into locks vs. open knobs, and optionally a character bible / handoff
        2. Sparks — anti-generic visual design: prompt rules, a small design-decision sparks DB, and image decomposition so you can iterate one component at a time instead of re-rolling the whole character
        3. Draft economy — make generations count: match model/tool to the look, draft cheap before finishing, spend compute/credits where it pays
    
        On Hermes: point the agent at the repo and ask it to install the skills — it can handle the clone/symlink setup for you.
    
        https://github.com/schorjonathan-code/character-design-agentic-workflows
    
        Looking for feedback — what works, what’s confusing, what you’d want next.
